Common Carrier Exemption
PSX is classified as a "common carrier" under the TCPA and courts have recently held that providers such as PSX are protected by the common carrier exemption in the TCPA. In Rinky Dink Inc. v. Electronic Merchant Systems Inc. (W.D. Wash. Feb. 24, 2015),the court held that the TCPA does not apply to common carriers in the call and text message context. The TCPA was intended to ‘apply to the persons initiating the telephone call or sending the message and ... not to the common carrier ... that transmits the call or messages and that is not the originator or controller of the content of the call or message.’ Rinky Dink (citing Couser v. Pre-paid Legal Services Inc., 994 F.Supp.2d 1100, 1104 (S.D.Cal.2014)). You are the person initiating the call, not PSX, so please make sure that your SMS and outbound dialing practices are TCPA compliant.

Prior Express Written Consent (PEWC)
If you use PSX to send SMS texts or outbound sales calls, you must have PEWC from each person who will be called or texted. If you are unsure of whether the PEWC records you have constitute "written" or "consent", call attorney John Cox at 1-800-400-6968.

Don't Hide Behind the Autodialer
Assume that the PSX platform has the ability to autodial, even if our technology does not. What constitutes an "autodialer" or the capability to autodial is still being hashed out in courtrooms today. Let's not test the waters.
